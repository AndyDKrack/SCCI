Street Children Care Initiative (SCCI)

The Street Children Care Initiative (SCCI) was founded in 2017 in Juba, South Sudan by a group of South Sudanese intellectuals who feel concerned with the way South Sudanese Street kids grieves on and in streets of all the towns of South Sudan. The idea is predestined to elevate the lives of these street children through integrating them back to the society, skills and talent promotion activities. SCCI is striving to ensure that the lives of women, men, boys and girls become sustainable in our society.

Street Children Care Initiative is registered as National, non-governmental and non-profit organization by the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission (RRC) of the Republic of South Sudan with it Registration Number 3758 in 2021. The SCCI is non-confessional, not for profit, non-governmental, non-political, non-partisan, non-discriminatory agency for which focus on humanitarian and development sectors. SCCI is a registered NNGO in South Sudan by RRC, the South Sudanese governmental institution. The Relief and Rehabilitation Commission is being established and governed in its functions for registering and overseeing the NNGOs and INGOs in Republic of South Sudan by the South Sudanese legal act “RRC Act” of 2016. The RRC is a governmental institution under the oversight of the Minister of Humanitarian Affairs and Disaster Management, Republic of South Sudan.

Mandate, Mission and Values

The SCCI NNGO engages in South Sudan child’s families, communities, society through holistic approach.

  • To empower children, young people and communities where street and most socially underprivileged children, adolescents and youth are form to make vital changes that tackle the root causes of discrimination, exclusion and vulnerability.
  • To drive change in practice and policy at sub national and national levels through our reach, building capacity and advocacy of the realities children, adolescents and youth face.
  • To work with children, adolescents, youth and communities to make them resilient through cross-sectoral activities.
  • To make inclusive peacebuilding with youth’s voices and participation.

    Our Causes

    The SCCI takes a family – based and community-based approach while addressing child needs. We do multisectoral and integrated approach to ensure that children receive sustainable quality services, and favorable environment.

    Food security & Livelihood

    Clean drinking water, Food Security and Livelihood: Improved well-being of children in targeted households areas with resilience and access to sustainable sources of income

    GESI and Gender Base Violence (GBV) advocacy

    Engaging households and communities and carry out sensitization about the manifestations of gender-based violence and its impact on household, community awareness and emphasis on how to minimize and eliminate it. Thie project is currently being funded by USAID through DT global in Leer County Unity State.

    Child Protection

    Engaging local community members and liaise with them to draw appropriate measures to ensure that children are protected from danger and abuse. We will also sensitize them on children’s rights.

    Shelter & Non-Food Item (S-NFI)

    Shelter & Non-Food Item (S-NFI): This program aims to provide safe shelter for internally displaced persons by providing healthy living conditions with privacy and dignity to groups of people and families in South Sudan. This occurs in various forms base on urgent need of the people displaced by violent conflict. Other components of the program include the distribution of non-food items for daily life such as seasonal clothing, cooking equipment, shelter kits and plastic sheets.

    Human Rights and Social Justice

    Emphasizing on the respect for human rights and social justice as enshrined in the South Sudan constitution and other human rights instruments. We engage community leaders and local residents in sensitization workshops about people’s rights and the benefits of ensuring that they are respected.

    WASH

    WASH by repairing, rehabilitation of boreholes, and awareness campaigns on key lifesaving messages (health and hygiene messages, good sanitation practices).
